The input is a single natural-language text string containing arbitrary written content.
The input may be any kind of prose such as articles, blog posts, emails, transcripts, lists, or mixed formats.
The text may be in any language, including multilingual or code-switched segments.
The text may contain company names, person names, topics, and themes, but it is valid even if none are present.
The text may include punctuation, numbers, dates, times, currencies, URLs, email addresses, hashtags, mentions, emojis, and other special characters.
The text may include quotations, headers, footers, citations, references, or semi-structured elements rendered as plain text.
The input may range in length from a single word to a long multi-paragraph document.
The text may be empty or whitespace-only.
The text is treated as plain textual content regardless of embedded markup like HTML, markdown, or JSON snippets.
The input does not require any specific structure beyond being a text passage.
